The Mediocrity of the Jason Garrett Era

There is no better example of a mediocrity in the NFL, and even in life, than the one and only Jason Garrett. A man who is better known for his clapping abilities, than his coaching abilities. However, if we take out his 1st half season as the Cowboys head coach (he took over for Wade Phillips 8 games into the season). Also, if we just take out the last two years with the emergence of rookie sensations, Ezekiel Elliot and Dak Prescott, Garrett's record is a perfect 40-40.
